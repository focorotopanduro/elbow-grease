This is the first post on the Beit Building Contractors blog. It's a placeholder while the infrastructure ships — the real flagship post (a complete walkthrough of filing a Florida hurricane roof insurance claim) lands shortly after.

## Why we're writing

After 12+ years roofing and building in Central Florida, we hear the same questions over and over. Most don't have a clear answer online — or the answer that's online is surface-level, written by someone who's never been on a Florida roof in August. We want to fix that.

The blog will cover:

- **Insurance claims** — what carriers actually pay for, what they don't, and how to document a claim that gets approved.
- **Hurricane preparedness** — pre-season inspections, mitigation upgrades, what survives 130 mph wind.
- **Materials guides** — when tile beats shingle, when metal beats both, when standing-seam is worth the cost.
- **Florida-specific construction** — code interpretations, permit fluency, the things that matter in Orange and Seminole counties specifically.

## What's next

Posts will publish on a roughly weekly cadence once the flagship lands. If there's a question you've been asking and not finding a good answer to, [let us know via the contact form](/#contact) — we're more likely to write the post if we know the question is real.
