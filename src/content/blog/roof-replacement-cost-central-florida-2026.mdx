Roof replacement is one of the largest single home expenses most Florida homeowners ever face — and the actual price varies enormously by material, square footage, decking condition, county permit office, and the specific scope items each contractor includes (or hides) in their quote.

This post is the transparent breakdown we wish was available when we started Beit Building Contractors years ago. Real cost ranges. Real Central Florida 2026 pricing. Real permit-fee differences across Orange, Seminole, and Osceola counties. And the line-by-line quote anatomy that lets you compare two contractors apples-to-apples instead of guessing which one is "padding."

> **Beit Tip:** Every cost figure in this post is presented as a **range, flagged as typical Central FL 2026 pricing**. There is no such thing as "the average roof cost" — actual price depends on your specific home. Use these ranges to evaluate whether quotes you receive are reasonable, not to budget down to the dollar.

## The cost variables (what actually drives the number)

Five variables determine 90% of the price:

1. **Material category** — tile vs shingle vs metal vs flat membrane (full breakdown in our [Florida Roofing Materials Guide](/blog/florida-roofing-materials-guide-2026))
2. **Roof size** — measured in "squares" (1 square = 100 sq ft of roof surface). A typical Florida residential roof is 18-30 squares; large homes can hit 40-50.
3. **Complexity** — number of slopes, valleys, dormers, skylights, chimneys, multi-pitch transitions. A simple gable roof is 30-40% cheaper to install than a complex hip-and-valley roof of the same square footage.
4. **Decking condition** — discovered after tear-off. Typical contingency budget: replacement allowance for ~10% of decking. Larger discoveries are quoted as change orders.
5. **Code-required upgrades** — FBC 7th Edition (2024) requires specific flashing, ventilation, and (in HVHZ counties) FBC §1518 secondary water barrier. Each adds line-item cost.

Two homes on the same Orlando street can vary by 50% in re-roof cost based on these variables alone.

## Tile costs in Central Florida (concrete vs clay)

Tile dominates older Florida neighborhoods — the Mediterranean Revival and Spanish Colonial homes throughout [Orlando's Park Avenue district, Audubon Park, and College Park](/winter-park-roofing). It's also standard on much of Central Florida's high-end new construction (Lake Nona estates, Dr. Phillips luxury sections).

**Typical Central FL 2026 ranges:**

- **Concrete tile installed:** $8-15 per sq ft (so a 22-square home: ~$17,600-$33,000)
- **Clay tile installed:** $12-25 per sq ft (same 22 squares: ~$26,400-$55,000)

**Cost factors:**

- **Profile** — S-tile, flat shake, and barrel profiles have different per-square-foot pricing. Barrel and S-tile are typically 10-20% more than flat profiles.
- **Color lot availability** — historic / discontinued color matches drive cost up substantially. Color-matching for a 30-year-old Park Avenue tile roof can require sourcing through specialty distributors.
- **Structural verification** — older homes (pre-1980) sometimes need a licensed engineer's letter ($300-600) before a tile re-roof to confirm structural capacity for tile's 600-1,100 lbs per 100 sq ft weight.
- **Brands available in Central FL:** Westlake Royal Roofing (formerly Boral), Eagle Roofing Products, Crown Roof Tiles. Each has Florida distribution; lead times vary 2-8 weeks depending on profile + color.

> **Beit Tip:** If your tile roof leaks, your tiles often DON'T need replacement — the underlayment beneath does. A "tile re-roof" in Florida typically reuses ~60-80% of the existing tile and replaces only the underlayment and broken pieces, which substantially reduces cost compared to full new-tile pricing. Get this scoped specifically.

## Shingle costs (architectural is the standard)

Architectural (laminate / dimensional) shingles are the workhorse of newer Florida subdivisions. 3-tab is largely obsolete here — many carriers won't insure homes with 3-tab roofs, and the cost difference vs architectural is small enough that 3-tab rarely makes economic sense.

**Typical Central FL 2026 ranges:**

- **Architectural shingle installed (basic / 25-year line):** $4-6 per sq ft (22 squares: $8,800-$13,200)
- **Architectural premium / designer (GAF Timberline HDZ, Owens Corning Duration, CertainTeed Landmark Pro):** $5-8 per sq ft
- **Class 4 impact-resistant (UL 2218):** add $0.75-1.50 per sq ft to whichever line you choose
- **3-tab shingle (rarely recommended):** $3-5 per sq ft

**Cost factors:**

- **Wind class.** ASTM D7158 Class G (130 mph) is minimum for Orange County's design wind speed; Class H (150 mph) is the upgrade. Class H typically adds 5-10% to material cost. Shop both options at quote time.
- **Algae resistance.** GAF Timberline AS, CertainTeed Northgate — algae-resistant lines extend cosmetic life by 3-5 years against Florida's humidity-driven streaking. Typically adds $0.30-0.60 per sq ft. Worth it.
- **Class 4 impact discount.** Most Florida carriers offer a 5-15% premium discount on the wind-and-hail portion when you install UL 2218 Class 4 impact-resistant shingles. Over the shingle's lifetime, the premium savings often exceed the upgrade cost.

For full material context including which shingle wins for which home type, see our [Florida Roofing Materials Guide](/blog/florida-roofing-materials-guide-2026).

## Metal costs

Metal roofing splits into two distinct categories with very different pricing:

**Typical Central FL 2026 ranges:**

- **Exposed-fastener metal (5V crimp, R-panel, M-loc):** $8-12 per sq ft (22 squares: $17,600-$26,400)
- **Standing-seam metal:** $12-20 per sq ft (22 squares: $26,400-$44,000)

**Cost factors:**

- **Panel gauge.** 24-gauge panels cost more than 26-gauge but last longer in coastal exposures. For inland Central Florida, 26-gauge is typically sufficient.
- **Coating.** Galvalume (zinc-aluminum-magnesium) is standard; Kynar 500 painted finish adds 15-30%. PVDF / Kynar is what carries the long color warranties (30+ years).
- **Trim + accessories.** Standing-seam costs vary substantially with the number of trim pieces (gable trim, ridge cap, pipe penetrations, valley flashing). Complex roofs with many transitions can push cost toward the high end of the range.
- **Brands available in Central FL:** McElroy Metal (full lineup), MBCI, Petersen Aluminum (Pac-Clad for premium standing-seam), ATAS International. All have Florida distributors; lead times typically 4-8 weeks for standing-seam.

> **Beit Tip:** Standing-seam metal is the longest-lifespan residential roof system available — typically 50-75+ years vs 18-25 for architectural shingle. The cost premium spread over the lifespan often makes metal **cheaper per year** than asphalt, even though the upfront price looks higher. If you're 50+ years old and don't plan to fund another re-roof in your lifetime, this math is worth running.

## Flat / low-slope costs (additions, screen rooms, light commercial)

For roof pitches below 2:12 (typical of additions, screen-room roofs, garages, light commercial), shingle and tile aren't options — membrane systems take over.

**Typical Central FL 2026 ranges:**

- **TPO (thermoplastic polyolefin):** $5-8 per sq ft installed
- **EPDM (rubber membrane):** $4-7 per sq ft installed
- **Modified bitumen:** $4-8 per sq ft installed

For Central Florida residential additions, **TPO is usually the right answer** — its white reflective surface stays cooler in summer (reducing attic heat load), 20-30 year lifespan rivals most pitched-roof systems, and Florida UV exposure tolerance is well-documented.

## Add-on costs that catch homeowners off-guard

The above per-square-foot prices typically include tear-off, dump fees, basic flashing, ridge cap, and standard underlayment. The line items that sometimes show up as separate costs:

- **Decking replacement (when discovered).** $1.50-3.00 per sq ft on affected area. Budget a 10% allowance in your contract — anything beyond that becomes a documented change order.
- **Fascia and soffit repair.** $5-15 per linear foot. Common in older Florida homes where humidity has compromised the wood.
- **Ridge vents.** $3-8 per linear foot installed. Critical for proper attic ventilation; sometimes itemized separately.
- **Drip edge + flashing.** Usually included in the per-sq-ft price; if line-itemed, ~$300-600 for a typical residential job.
- **FBC §1518 secondary water barrier.** Self-adhered SBS underlayment. $0.50-1.50 per sq ft. **Increasingly standard in Central Florida quotes**; if not included, ask why. The post-Ian / post-Nicole era has made SWB the de-facto standard even outside HVHZ counties.
- **Permit + inspection** — see next section.
- **Disposal / dump fees** — usually included in the per-sq-ft installed price. If line-itemed: $300-700 typical.
- **Skylight reseal or replacement** — $400-2,000 per skylight depending on replacement vs reseal.
- **Chimney flashing** — $500-1,500 for hand-fabricated copper or lead-coated copper on historic homes.

> **Beit Tip:** When comparing two quotes, **normalize them line-by-line**. If Quote A says "labor + materials: $14,000" and Quote B itemizes 12 separate line items totaling $13,800, Quote B is the safer signal — the contractor has thought through the scope and committed to it in writing. Vague lump-sum quotes are how mid-project surprises happen.

## Permit + inspection fees by county

Florida residential roof replacement requires a permit. Fees vary by county and project value:

**Orange County (unincorporated + most of Orlando metro):**

- Typical residential reroof permit: $200-700
- Includes: permit issuance + 1 inspection
- Re-inspection fees if first inspection fails: ~$100 per
- Find current fee schedule at [ocfl.net](https://www.ocfl.net/) Building Department

**City of Orlando (within city limits):**

- Separate from Orange County — pulls through Orlando Building Department
- Typical residential reroof permit: $250-600

**Seminole County (Oviedo, Sanford, Lake Mary, Casselberry):**

- Typical residential reroof permit: $200-600
- Find at [seminolecountyfl.gov](https://www.seminolecountyfl.gov/) Building Division

**Osceola County (Kissimmee, St. Cloud):**

- Typical residential reroof permit: $150-500

**Winter Park (within city limits):**

- Separate from Orange County
- Typical residential reroof permit: $250-600
- **Historic district homes** (Park Avenue, Olde Winter Park, Lake Sue) require Historic Preservation Board review — adds 2-4 weeks lead time and a separate review fee

A reputable Central FL contractor includes the permit cost as a line item in the quote. If a quote bundles "permit fees" into a vague total without specifying which county, ask for clarification — permit cost is fixed and easy to itemize.

## How to read a quote like a pro

A good Florida roof replacement quote has all of these line items, even if they're grouped under section headers:

- **Tear-off + disposal** (or noted "included")
- **Underlayment** — specifically: FBC §1518 SWB or basic synthetic
- **Decking allowance** (typical: 10% of total slopes, replaceable per change order if more is needed)
- **Roofing material** — by brand and product line, not just generic ("GAF Timberline HDZ in Charcoal," not "asphalt shingle")
- **Wind class** — ASTM D7158 G or H
- **Drip edge, ridge cap, ridge vent, valley flashing, sidewall flashing, plumbing vent boots**
- **Pipe penetrations** itemized
- **Skylight handling** (reseal or replace)
- **Chimney flashing** (especially for historic homes)
- **Permit fees** — county specified
- **Final cleanup**
- **Workmanship warranty** — duration + what's covered + what voids it
- **Manufacturer warranty registration** — done by the contractor on your behalf
- **Payment schedule** — deposit + progress + balance

Lump-sum quotes ("labor and materials: $X") are a yellow flag. Itemized quotes give you the scope clarity needed to compare contractors and protect against mid-project change orders. > **Beit Tip:** The honest signal of a good quote is **specificity**. Specific shingle brand. Specific wind class. Specific underlayment product. Specific permit fee. Specific decking allowance. Specific warranty terms. Vagueness in any of these areas is where quote-padding lives.

Premium upgrade scenarios:
