Picking a roof material in Florida is not the same decision homeowners face anywhere else in the country. Year-round UV cooks asphalt at twice the rate of northern climates. Daily summer thunderstorms test every flashing seam. Hurricane season puts every fastener under structural load. And Florida's building code (FBC 7th Edition / 2024) sets stricter wind-resistance standards than most homeowners realize, including the FBC §1518 secondary water barrier requirement that affects every reroof in the state.

This guide walks through the four roofing materials a Central Florida homeowner is realistically choosing between in 2026 — concrete and clay tile, asphalt shingles, metal, and flat membrane systems — with honest cost ranges, lifespan expectations, hurricane performance, and the decision framework we use when a homeowner asks "which one should I pick for my house?"

If a hurricane recently damaged your roof and you're trying to choose a replacement material as part of an [insurance claim](/blog/florida-hurricane-roof-insurance-claim-guide), the calculus shifts — your carrier may dictate certain material categories. We address that in the Insurance Angle section.

> **Beit Tip:** No single material is "best" for every Florida home. The right answer depends on your home's age, structural capacity, neighborhood architecture, hurricane priority, and budget. Anyone who tells you "shingle is always best" or "metal is always best" is selling, not advising.

## Why Florida is different from anywhere else

Three factors compound on every Florida roof:

1. **UV exposure.** Florida averages 230+ sunny days per year. UV degrades asphalt at roughly 2x the rate of comparable Mid-Atlantic states. A 30-year shingle in Pennsylvania is realistically a 20-22 year shingle in Central Florida.
2. **Thermal cycling.** Daily summer attic temperatures over 140°F followed by afternoon thunderstorms create thermal-shock stress on every roofing material, especially organic-fiber shingles and uncoated metal fasteners.
3. **Hurricane wind loads.** Orange County and most of Central Florida sits in the ASCE 7-22 wind-design zone for 130 mph 3-second gusts. The Florida Building Code requires every new roof to meet that load, with stricter requirements in HVHZ counties (Miami-Dade, Broward) where the design wind speed jumps to 150-180 mph.

Every material below is rated against these three factors specifically, not generic national lifespans.

## Concrete and clay tile

Tile dominates older Florida neighborhoods — the Mediterranean Revival and Spanish Colonial homes throughout [Orlando's Audubon Park, College Park, and Park Avenue districts](/orlando-roofing). It's also the standard on much of Central Florida's high-end new construction.

**Concrete vs clay.** Both are heavy, both last decades, both perform exceptionally in hurricane wind events. Concrete is roughly 60% the cost of clay and slightly more impact-resistant. Clay holds its color longer (it's mineral-based, not surface-coated) and has the period-correct authenticity for historic homes.

**Lifespan.** 50+ years for clay (many original 1920s tile roofs in Winter Park are still on the home). 40-50 years for concrete with proper maintenance.

**Hurricane performance.** With proper installation per FBC §1518 (secondary water barrier under the tile), tile is among the most hurricane-resilient systems available. The common failure mode is wind-driven rain entering between tiles when no SWB is installed — this is why FBC §1518 mandates SWB in HVHZ counties and most Central Florida insurers strongly favor it elsewhere.

**Weight consideration.** Tile weighs 600-1,100 lbs per 100 sq ft. Older homes (pre-1980) sometimes need structural verification before a tile install or re-tile — a licensed engineer's letter can cost $300-600 but prevents a much more expensive surprise mid-project.

**Cost range, Central Florida 2026.** Concrete tile installed: $8-15 per sq ft. Clay tile installed: $12-25 per sq ft. Pricing varies significantly with tile profile (S-tile vs flat shake vs barrel) and color lot availability.

**Brands we work with.** Westlake Royal Roofing (the parent of the former Boral tile lines), Eagle Roofing Products, Crown Roof Tiles. Each has a Central Florida distributor network with reasonable lead times.

> **Beit Tip:** If your tile roof is leaking, do NOT immediately assume the tiles are the problem. The vast majority of "tile roof" leaks are actually underlayment failures — and the underlayment is what FBC §1518 addresses. A proper tile re-roof replaces the underlayment system; the tiles themselves often get reused.

## Asphalt shingles

Architectural (also called dimensional or laminate) shingles are the workhorse of newer Florida subdivisions — Lake Nona, MetroWest, [Live Oak Reserve in Oviedo](/oviedo-roofing), Stoneybrook. 3-tab shingles are largely obsolete in Florida; many carriers will not insure homes with 3-tab roofs and the cost difference vs. architectural is small enough that it rarely makes sense.

**Wind class.** Asphalt shingles are rated under ASTM D7158 by Class F (110 mph), Class G (130 mph), or Class H (150 mph). For Orange County's 130 mph design wind speed, Class G is the minimum and Class H is the better choice. This is printed on every shingle bundle's wrapper — verify it before installation begins.

**Impact rating.** UL 2218 rates shingles on a 1-4 scale for impact resistance. Class 4 ("impact resistant") shingles often qualify for an insurance discount with most Florida carriers — typically 5-15% off the wind-and-hail portion of the policy. Over the shingle's lifetime, the discount can offset most of the Class 4 cost premium.

**Lifespan in Florida.** Architectural shingles labeled 30-year by the manufacturer realistically deliver 18-25 years of service in Central Florida due to UV degradation. Algae-resistant lines (GAF Timberline AS, CertainTeed Northgate) extend cosmetic life by another 3-5 years by preventing the dark streaks Florida humidity invites.

**Cost range, Central Florida 2026.** Architectural shingles installed: $4-7 per sq ft including tear-off, dump fee, and FBC §1518 SWB. Premium designer shingles (GAF Timberline HDZ, Owens Corning TruDefinition Duration) at the high end. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles add roughly $0.75-1.50 per sq ft.

**Brands we work with.** GAF (the most common in Central Florida — full Timberline lineup), Owens Corning (Duration and Berkshire lines), CertainTeed (Landmark and Northgate). All three honor full warranty terms when installed by a properly-credentialed contractor.

> **Beit Tip:** The difference between a 15-year shingle install and a 25-year install on the same home, with the same product, is the underlayment + ventilation system underneath. Pay attention to what's UNDER the shingle, not just the shingle itself.

## Metal

Metal roofing in Florida splits into two categories with very different installs:

**Standing-seam.** Vertical panels with concealed fasteners, joined by raised seams that interlock. Premium product, premium price, premium performance. This is what high-end coastal homes and modern-architecture custom builds use.

**Exposed-fastener (5V crimp, R-panel, M-loc).** Horizontal panels with screws driven through the panel face. Lower cost, faster install, suitable for outbuildings, agricultural structures, and budget-conscious primary homes. The exposed fasteners are the failure point — gasket washers degrade in Florida UV and need re-tightening every 8-12 years.

**Lifespan.** Standing-seam: 50-70+ years (some manufacturers offer 100-year structural warranties). Exposed-fastener: 30-50 years with fastener maintenance, less without.

**Hurricane performance.** Standing-seam metal is among the best-performing roof systems in 150+ mph wind events when installed with proper clip spacing and underlayment per FBC. Exposed-fastener systems perform well too, but the failure mode is fastener pull-through rather than panel failure.

**Heat reflectivity.** Metal roofs reflect significantly more solar radiation than asphalt. An Energy Star-rated metal roof can reduce attic temperatures by 10-25°F, which translates to lower cooling costs. The exact savings depend on attic ventilation and insulation, but the effect is real.

**Cost range, Central Florida 2026.** Standing-seam metal installed: $12-20 per sq ft, depending on panel profile and gauge. Exposed-fastener metal installed: $8-12 per sq ft.

**Brands we work with.** McElroy Metal (full standing-seam and exposed-fastener lineup, Florida distribution), MBCI, ATAS International, Petersen Aluminum (Pac-Clad for standing-seam). For coastal exposures, marine-grade Galvalume coating is the standard.

## Flat and low-slope membrane systems

For roof slopes below 2:12 (typical of additions, screen-room roofs, garages, and most commercial buildings), shingle and tile aren't options. Membrane systems take over.

**TPO (thermoplastic polyolefin).** Single-ply white membrane, heat-welded seams. Most common modern flat-roof system. Reflective, energy-efficient, 20-30 year lifespan. **Cost: $5-8 per sq ft.**

**EPDM (rubber membrane).** Single-ply black membrane, glued or mechanically fastened. Time-tested system, especially for residential additions. 20-25 year lifespan. **Cost: $4-7 per sq ft.**

**Modified bitumen.** Asphalt-based torch-down or self-adhered system. Older technology but still common for repair-grade work. 15-20 year lifespan. **Cost: $4-8 per sq ft.**

For Central Florida residential additions, TPO is usually the right answer — its reflectivity helps with attic temperatures, the white surface stays cooler in summer, and 25-year lifespan rivals most pitched-roof systems.

## Decision matrix: which material wins for your home

Use these tiebreakers when you're between two options:

| Your priority | Best choice | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Maximum lifespan | Standing-seam metal or clay tile | 50-70+ years; expect to replace once in your lifetime |
| Lowest upfront cost | Architectural shingle | $4-7/sq ft installed; most universal |
| Best hurricane resilience | Standing-seam metal or tile (with FBC §1518 SWB) | Both rated for 150+ mph with proper install |
| Lowest maintenance | Standing-seam metal | No annual upkeep, no algae streaks, no fastener tightening |
| Period-correct historic home | Clay tile | Required for [Park Avenue / Olde Winter Park district](/winter-park-roofing) and many HOA jurisdictions |
| Energy efficiency / cool roof | Metal or Energy Star reflective shingle | 10-25°F attic temperature reduction documented |
| Insurance discount eligibility | Class 4 impact-resistant shingle | 5-15% premium discount with most FL carriers |
| Quick install (timeline-pressed) | Architectural shingle | 1-3 days typical residential; tile + metal are 3-7 days |

## Maintenance basics

Every roof, regardless of material, lasts longer with annual or biannual care. The basics:

- **Annual visual inspection.** From the ground with binoculars after every named storm and every May-June. Look for displaced shingles/tiles, lifted flashing, rust streaks on metal.
- **Gutter cleaning.** Twice annually minimum. Clogged gutters back water under the roofing edge, which is how most "interior leak" tickets actually start.
- **Tree trimming.** Limbs within 10 feet of the roof are the #1 cause of impact damage during named storms.
- **Attic check.** Once or twice per year — look for daylight visible through decking (a problem), water staining (a problem), and excessive heat (ventilation problem).

> **Beit Tip:** A 20-minute annual roof check can extend a roof's service life by 3-5 years. Most homeowners never do it. The contractors who push proactive inspections (we offer them free in our [Orlando service area](/orlando-roofing)) typically catch issues before they become claims.

## Realistic cost ranges (Central Florida 2026)

The numbers above are for typical residential roofs sized 18-30 squares (1,800-3,000 sq ft of roof surface). For a complete picture on a specific home, the variables that move price meaningfully:

- **Decking replacement.** Old or damaged plywood/OSB decking discovered after tear-off: $1.50-3.00 per sq ft additional.
- **Fascia and soffit repair.** Common in older Florida homes: $5-15 per linear foot.
- **Permit costs.** Vary by county and project value: typically $200-800 in Orange County, similar in Seminole.
- **Tear-off and dump.** Already included in the per-sq-ft installed prices above for shingle and metal — sometimes a separate line item for tile.
- **FBC §1518 secondary water barrier.** Increasingly standard; if it's not included in your quote, ask why. Adds $0.50-1.50 per sq ft for self-adhered SBS.

> **Beit Tip:** A reputable Florida contractor's quote should be itemised line-by-line with explicit line items for: tear-off, FBC §1518 SWB, decking allowance, flashing replacement, ridge/soffit ventilation, and disposal. Vague "labor and materials: $X" quotes are how short-pay surprises happen.
